Sound Alert Systems for Deaf Drivers

As autonomous vehicles (AVs) become more prevalent, concerns arise about their impact on people with hearing impairments. This paper presents an approach to improve driving safety for this group using assistive technologies (ATs). We explore hardware upgrades, machine learning for sound classification, direction-finding algorithms, Arduino microcontroller use, ROS integration, and user interface development. Our system aims to detect hazards in real-time, enhancing driving for those with hearing impairments. We also suggest future improvements like phase difference analysis and customized dataset creation. This work contributes to inclusive transportation solutions.
